I am a second-year Master (MPhil) student in Computer Science at Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (HKUST) and a member of the Human-Computer Interaction Lab, advised by Prof. Xiaojuan Ma.

My research interests lie at the intersection of Computational Social Science and Natural Language Processing (especially Text Mining). I aim to leverage large-scale online social data to derive actionable and generalizable insights about our lives through advanced methods from HCI and NLP, and to build intelligent systems for improving our well-being.

Currently, I am working on projects related to 1) online collective behavior of emerging groups (e.g., disaster victims) in crisis events, and 2) knowledge bases construction for subjective experience. During the previous year, I was fortunate to work with Prof. Diyi Yang at Georgia Tech and Prof. Alon Halevy at Facebook AI.
