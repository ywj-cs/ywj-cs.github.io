I am a second-year Master (MPhil) student in Computer Science at Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (HKUST). My advisor is Prof. Xiaojuan Ma.

My research interests lie in Computational Social Science and Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence. My goal is to gain a deep understanding of misinformation, toxic language, and anti-social behavior online, and to create AI-powered technologies to combat these challenges for the benefit of people's wellbeing. I employ advanced NLP models to identify human behaviors and use mixed methods (e.g., large-scale data analysis and content analysis) based on theories (e.g., persuasion) in my work.

Currently, I am working on 1) understanding how vulnerable groups seek healthcare assistance through social media during COVID-19, and 2) modeling subjective language to improve search systems. I was fortunate to work with Prof. Diyi Yang at Georgia Tech and Prof. Alon Halevy at Facebook AI during the previous year.
